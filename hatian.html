There have three major moments where Haitian people have migrated from their homeland to areas of America. One such migration happened around the time of 1804 when Haitian people won their independence from French colonization. This revolution disrupted much of the current economy, and caused many wealthy French and Haitian people invested in that current economy to migrate to other parts of the world. One of many destinations for these different groups of people was the already established francophone area of Louisiana as well as parts of Florida.
